Illusion of Weight in Sports

Kunj Golwala, Diego Abarcar-Calugay, Aditya Sarkar, Dannuo Li,
University of Maryland College Park,
Report Code Data

Illusion of weight across different sports.

Brief abstract

The integration of Virtual Reality (VR) in athletic training is currently limited by standard, lightweight controllers that fail to provide realistic force feedback across different sports equipment. To address this without relying on expensive mechanical hardware, this project investigates pseudo-haptic feedback to dynamically simulate weight in a competitive VR sports context. By manipulating the Control-Display (C/D) ratio to introduce visual lag, we aim to induce a visual-kinesthetic illusion of weight. We are developing a unified Unity environment featuring Table Tennis, Tennis, and Baseball, applying distinct C/D gains of 1.0, 0.85, and 0.70 to represent light, medium, and heavy equipment, respectively. Additionally, we will explore alternatives like Maximum Velocity Restriction and visual deformation cues. A localized user study will evaluate perceived heaviness to statistically validate the pseudo-haptic illusion's effectiveness for XR sports training.
